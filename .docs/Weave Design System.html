§ 01 — Concept

The name is the system.

“Weave” names the craft and the object. Warp and weft, threads that cross — a quiet metaphor for how software, like fabric, is made from many small repeated choices.

Principle 01

Editorial, not loud.

Generous whitespace. Real serifs. The confidence to leave things unsaid. Voice is quiet and considered, like a small press.

Principle 02

Warm, not beige.

A cream paper ground, terracotta warmth, and oxblood for gravity. Colors share chroma so the palette hangs together.

Principle 03

Woven, not gradiented.

Structure shows. Grids, hairlines, tabular numbers. Texture comes from rhythm, not gloss — no drop shadows where a rule will do.

§ 02 — Wordmark

Wordmark & glyph.

A lowercase wordmark paired with a glyph drawn as a single running stitch — one line, five peaks.

Products use a capitalized suffix set in accent color.
Clear space

Reserve at minimum the x-height of the mark on every side. The glyph may stand alone at sizes under 24px where the wordmark becomes illegible.

Do not

Do not stretch, re-color outside the palette, add effects, enclose in shapes, or pair with other display serifs. Never uppercase the wordmark.

§ 03 — Color

Warm, considered, oklch-defined.

Two palettes — Loom (cream, daylight) and Thread (oxblood, dusk) — share the same accent hues at adjusted lightness.
